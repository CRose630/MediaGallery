Alstroemeria

×

Alstroemeria


Also known as the Peruvian lily or the lily of the Incas, this flower is native to South America. These flowers symbolize friendship and devotion.

Bleeding Heart

Romance, Purity, and Innocence
×

Bleeding Heart


The bleeding heart most often symbolizes romance, while the white version of this flower symbloizes innocence and purity. The white flower often represents young women who have died tragically.

Columbine

Faith, Innocence, and Foolishness
×

Columbine


The columbine has had a multitude of meaning over the centuries. The root of its name means dove, while the root of its latin name, Aquilegia, translates to eagle.

Dahlia

Commitment and Perseverance
×

Dahlia


The dahlia was used to represent a lasting bond between two people by the Victorians. However, in modern times it can be used to celebrate joining a new religion or starting a healthy habit.

English Bluebell

Humility, Gratitude, and Love
×

English Bluebell


These flowers are also called harebells in Scotland becuase it is believed that witches would transform into hares and hide amoong this type of flower.

Foxglove

Creativity and Confidence
×

Foxglove


In folklore, picking foxglove is said to offend faeries. This flower is also a source of the drug digitalis and is a popular food source for hummingbirds.

Geranium

Friendship, Folly, and Melancholy
×

Geranium


White geraniums were once believed to repel snakes, and geranium leaves can be dried to make tea and can be used to make a compress to relieve pain.

Hibiscus

Delicate Beauty and Fleeting Glory

×

Hibiscus


Claimed to help reduce blood pressure when used in teas, the hibiscus is also the state flower of Hawaii.

Iris

Faith, Hope, and Wisdom
×

Iris


This flower is named after the Greek goddess of rainbows, as it comes in a variety of colors. They can also be candied as a delicacy.

Jonquil

Desire and Sorrow
×

Jonquil


The meanings of the jonquil stem from a Greek myth. Proserpina was gathering lilies when she was kidnapped by the god of the underworld, Pluto. When she dropped the lilies, they turned into jonquils.

Marigold

Passion, Cruelty, and Jealousy
×

Marigold


Marigold blooms can be used to ease pain cause by bee stings. There is a superstition that states that picking marigolds can turn you into a drunkard.

Nymphaea

Peace and Purity
×

Nymphaea


The water lily is very prominent in both Buddhism and Hinduism and it represents the cycle of life and death.
